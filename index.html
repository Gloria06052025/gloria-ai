FIN 567 - FinTech Strategy Project

School of Business and Management • 3 Credits

Course Description

FinTech Strategy Project serves as the capstone for the FinTech course series, synthesizing knowledge from blockchain, machine learning, AI, big data, and disruptive financial models. Students explore real-world challenges and opportunities in the financial industry and apply innovative FinTech strategies to address them. Through case analysis and strategic planning, learners identify key trends, assess organizational needs, and develop actionable roadmaps for implementation. The course emphasizes critical thinking, collaboration, and practical problem-solving. By completing the main project, students gain hands-on experience in crafting impactful strategies that integrate cutting-edge FinTech solutions to drive measurable business outcomes.

Course Outcomes

This course will prepare students to:

CO.1 - Identify key challenges and opportunities in financial services where FinTech innovations can drive value. (FinTech Strategy)
CO.2 - Analyze a company's micro and macro-environment to determine how blockchain, AI, big data, or other technology solutions can address specific business needs. (FinTech Strategy)
CO.3 - Design an innovative FinTech strategy that integrates concepts from relevant technologies and disruptive financial models. (FinTech Strategy)
CO.4 - Evaluate the feasibility and potential risks of proposed FinTech solutions using strategic frameworks and evidence-based reasoning. (FinTech Strategy)
CO.5 - Develop a comprehensive implementation roadmap for a FinTech solution that includes resource allocation, risk mitigation strategies, and performance measurement criteria. (FinTech Strategy)

FinTech Strategy Project

70% of Final Grade

Innovative Solutions for Industry Challenges

Covers Course Outcomes: CO1-5

Objective:

Students will leverage insights and knowledge gained from the three preceding courses—Machine Learning, AI, and Big Data Usage in Finance, Crypto Assets, ICO's, and Disruptive Models, and Advances in Blockchain and Distributed Ledgers—to design an innovative, actionable, and effective strategy for addressing a real-world problem or opportunity in their selected company or scenario.

Assignment Description:

Students will act as a FinTech strategy consultant for a real-world company of their choice, approved by the instructor. The project requires the identification of a significant financial, operational, or market challenge or opportunity within the organization.

Project Steps

1

Select a Company or Scenario

  • Choose a company or industry scenario where FinTech innovation is relevant
  • Consult with a CityU librarian early in the term to refine your choice
  • Avoid large companies (e.g., Apple, Tesla) unless focusing on niche problems
  • Mid-size companies, startups, or industries in transformation are recommended
  • Submit a one-paragraph proposal for approval by Week 2
2

Research and Analyze

Conduct an in-depth analysis of the chosen company and its operating environment:

  • Contextual Analysis: Examine the company's business model, competitive position, and macro/micro-environment
  • Challenges/Opportunities: Identify and justify a specific pain point or opportunity where FinTech could provide value
  • Technical Prerequisites: Investigate the organization's current technological infrastructure
  • Data Acquisition Process: Outline how the company can acquire, manage, and secure required data
3

Develop and Justify the Strategy

  • Articulate the Problem/Opportunity
  • Propose an Innovative Solution and Supporting Infrastructure
  • Design the Strategic Framework using data-driven arguments
  • Utilize frameworks such as TWOS Matrix or SWOT
  • Conduct Cost-Benefit Analysis
4

Build an Implementation Plan

  • Resource Allocation: Outline technological, human, and financial resources
  • Operational Plan: Describe integration into existing processes
  • Risk Management: Identify risks and propose mitigation strategies
  • Measurement: Define KPIs to measure strategy success

Major Deliverables

Strategic Plan Document:

15-20 pages (excluding cover and reference pages). APA writing conventions with minimum of ten sources referenced and cited.

Presentation:

Professional, executive-level presentation (12-15 slides, approximately 15 minutes). Online students should record and submit narrated presentation.
